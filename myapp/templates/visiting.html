Most Visited Places in Kolkata

Victoria Memorial

The Victoria Memorial is a large marble monument on the Maidan in Central Kolkata, built between 1906 and 1921 by the British Raj. Dedicated to Queen Victoria, this iconic structure is surrounded by beautiful gardens.

More info

Howrah Bridge

The Howrah Bridge spans the Hooghly River and connects Kolkata with Howrah. As a famous symbol of Kolkata, this cantilever bridge is an engineering marvel and one of the busiest bridges in the world.

More info

Indian Museum

The Indian Museum is the largest and oldest museum in Asia, offering a vast collection of artifacts, fossils, and cultural exhibits. It’s a must-visit for history and culture enthusiasts.

More info

Dakshineswar Kali Temple

The Dakshineswar Kali Temple, situated on the eastern bank of the Hooghly River, is dedicated to Goddess Kali. Built by Rani Rashmoni, it’s a prominent pilgrimage site and known for its spiritual significance.

More info

Science City

Science City Kolkata is one of the largest science museums in the Indian subcontinent, offering interactive exhibits, a space theater, and fun science activities for all ages.

More info

Princep Ghat

Princep Ghat, located on the banks of River Hooghly, is a majestic structure famous for its Greek/ Gothic inlays. The beauty of Princep Ghat draws architecture and history buffs in large numbers. It was built in the year 1841, during the British Raj in India.
